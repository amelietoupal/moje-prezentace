Slide 4

Czech Food:
No Salads,
No Regrets.

🥩 Svíčková

Beef in creamy sauce with bread dumplings and cranberry. Tourists order it by pointing at the menu. In London this costs €35. Here it costs €6.

🧀 Smažený sýr

Fried cheese. Just cheese, fried. Tourists are confused. Czechs eat it all the time and are very proud of this.

🥐 Trdelník — Let's Address This

Not originally Czech. Every tourist buys one. Every Czech rolls their eyes. But everyone is happy so it is fine.

Full meal + beer: under €8 — tourists always think there is a mistake on the menu 🤯
Slide 5

Yes, We Also
Have Nature.
Stunning Nature.

Bohemian Switzerland

Big sandstone rocks, deep gorges, and misty forests. Narnia was filmed here. Yes, here in Czechia.

Moravian Wine Region

Beautiful vineyards and wine cellars in the hills. Moravians say it is the best wine in the world. They might be right.

Šumava

Deep forests, lakes, and real wolves. Tourists are always surprised about the wolves.

Krkonoše

Mountains for skiing in winter and hiking in summer. Home of the famous Czech legend Krakonoš.

Moravian Karst

Huge underground caves that look like something from a movie. Over 1,100 caves in total.

Slide 11 — Insider Tips

What Smart
Tourists Actually Do.

✅ DO THIS
  • 🚃 Take tram 22 through Prague — it goes past the Castle, Old Town, and river for 20 CZK. The best city tour that exists.
  • 🌅 Visit Charles Bridge at 6am. No tourists. Misty. Free. Completely different city.
  • 🍺 Order Kozel or Bernard in a local pub — not Staropramen. Locals will respect you instantly.
  • 🏰 Leave Prague for one day. Kutná Hora is 1 hour away. Český Krumlov is 3. Worth every minute.
❌ DON'T DO THIS
  • 🌭 Don't eat in Old Town Square restaurants. You're paying for the view, not the food. Walk 3 streets away.
  • 💸 Don't exchange money at airport kiosks. Use an ATM in the city. The difference is 20–30%.
  • 🧭 Don't only see Prague. That's like visiting Italy and only seeing the airport.
  • 🥐 Don't think trdelník is Czech. Just don't say it out loud near a local.
