UNESCO

Český Krumlov

Only 13,000 people live here. 2 million tourists visit every year. Locals either love the attention or have moved away. Both reactions are valid.

💀 Unique

Kutná Hora

A church made from 40,000 real human bones. A monk arranged them into decorations in the 1400s. Tourists think it is disturbing. Czechs think it is a perfectly normal school trip destination.

Underrated

Olomouc

Beautiful baroque city. Barely any tourists. Students from Palacký University know it is the best city in the country. Everyone else is still figuring it out.

🎬 Film Festival

Karlovy Vary

Hot springs, grand spa hotels, and a film festival since 1946. Also home of Becherovka — the liqueur that every Czech grandmother swears is medicine.

Beer Capital
of the World.
Officially.

Czechs drink 160 litres of beer per person per year. Tourists come here specifically for the beer. We just call it Wednesday.

Pilsner invented in Plzeň, 1842 🇨🇿
Original Budweiser? Czech. Not American.
Average pub price ~50 CZK. Yes really.

"In Czech pubs, a new beer arrives automatically when your glass is empty. You put a coaster on top to say no. Tourists never figure this out. Czechs sometimes pretend not to know either."

Slide 4

Czech Food:
No Salads,
No Regrets.

🥩 Svíčková

Beef in creamy sauce with bread dumplings and cranberry. The national dish. Every Czech has a strong opinion about whose grandmother makes it best. Tourists order it by pointing at the menu and cry a little when they see the price.

🧀 Smažený sýr

Fried cheese. Just cheese, fried. Tourists think it is a joke. Czechs eat it completely seriously, often at 2am after a night out, with tatarská omáčka. Zero regrets.

🥐 Trdelník — Let's Address This

Not originally Czech. Every tourist buys one thinking it is traditional. Every Czech sighs. We have accepted this is our cross to bear.

Full meal + beer: under €11 — tourists always think there is a mistake on the menu 🤯

Slide 5b

No Sea?
No Problem.

"Czechia is landlocked. We deal with it by having the best quarry lakes and reservoirs in Central Europe."

Quarry Lakes Former limestone quarries turned into crystal-clear swimming lakes. Water so blue tourists think it's photoshopped. Máchovo jezero · Hluboká · Plumlov
Reservoirs Lipno, Orlík, Nechranice — massive lakes with beaches, sailing and kayaking.
300+ natural & quarry swimming spots across the country "No ocean needed."
